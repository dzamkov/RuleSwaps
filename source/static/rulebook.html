Rules

RuleSwaps is a game where you create new rules as you play and compete to shift the rules in your favor. The core of RuleSwaps is a deck of cards where each card contains a fragment of an instruction. These cards can be assembled to build actions that are played out in the game. In RuleSwaps, every action taken, and every choice made, is written somewhere on a card.

Building Actions

There are three type of cards in RuleSwaps: Action, Condition and Player. To build an action, you'll need to start with an Action card. Sometimes, that's all you'll need, but in most cases, your card will have "slots" that need to be filled in using other cards. Slots appear as colored, parenthesized text. Read through your card. If you hit a slot, you'll need to place down another card whose type matches the slot. Then continue by reading through that card, again filling in slots as you go. Repeat the process until you've read through all the cards and filled in all the slots. You should end up with one long sentence that tells you what the action does.

Example: This action would be read as "If (You pay 20 coins), (Wealthiest player) loses 30 coins"

To "perform" an action, you would just follow the combined instructions from the cards.

The Constitution

So what do you actually do on your turn? That's entirely determined by a set of cards known as The Constitution. These are set up at the start of the game, and may be altered during play. The Constitution is made up of actions, called Amendments. Every round, each player will go through the amendments, in order, and perform the actions they describe (i.e. "invoking" the amendment). Setup

To start the game, all players get 20 coins. One player is chosen to go first and draws 4 cards. The next player going clockwise draws 5 cards and every subsequent player draws 6 cards. Afterwards, the game begins. Each player, starting with the first and going clockwise, invokes the amendments of constitution in the order they appear. This continues until a player wins.

Clarifications

  • If a card tells you to "perform an action", that means you build an action using cards from your hand and then perform that action. Afterwards, discard the cards used in the action.
  • If a card tells you to "propose an amendment", that means you build an action using cards from your hand and also specify a place in constitution where those cards would go. If the amendment is "ratified", those cards become a permanent addition to the constitution. Otherwise, the cards are discarded.
  • Whenever a player needs to discard, it is their choice which cards to discard.
